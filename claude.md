Surry (Taash) — Claude Context File
Overview
Surry is a 4-player, team-based trick-taking card game played with a standard 52-card deck. It is deeply social and strategic, with a unique mechanic where tricks are only secured if the same player wins two consecutive rounds.
Core Structure
Players
4 players
2 teams (opposite players are teammates)
Deck
Standard 52 cards
No jokers
Round Flow
1. Initial Deal
Dealer distributes 5 cards to each player
Distribution is anticlockwise
2. Bidding (Sarr)
Starts from player next to dealer
Allowed bids: 
Pass (8)
9, 10, 11, 12
Rules:
Each player must match or raise
If a player bids 12 → bidding ends immediately
Highest bidder wins
If all pass → first player controls
3. Trump Selection (Turup / Rang)
Highest bidder selects trump suit
If all pass → first player selects
4. Final Deal
Dealer gives 4 cards (round 1)
Dealer gives 4 cards (round 2)
Total = 13 cards per player
Gameplay
First Move
Bid winner starts the first trick
Card Ranking
A > K > Q > J > 10 > ... > 2
Trump Rules
Trump beats all other suits
Even lowest trump beats Ace of another suit
Follow Suit
Must follow suit if available
Trump Restriction
Can only play trump if player has no card of lead suit
Trick System (Core Mechanic)
Key Rule
Tricks are NOT immediately secured
All tricks go to a central pile
Securing Tricks
A player must win 2 consecutive tricks
Only then they collect ALL tricks in pile
Important
Must be same player (not teammate)
If streak breaks → pile continues growing
Objective
Teams aim to meet or exceed their bid (Sarr)
Only secured tricks count
Scoring (Piece System)
Success
Team scores number of tricks secured
Added to opponent's piece total
Failure
If bidding team fails: 
Penalty = 2 × bid
Added to their own piece total
Distributor (Dealer System)
Team with higher piece total becomes distributor
Distributor is considered a penalty role
Transfer Rule
Losing team must exceed current piece total to pass distributor role
Piece Accumulation
Pieces carry across rounds
Pressure builds over time
SOLD Rule
Limit
52 pieces
If exceeded
Team becomes SOLD
Piece resets to 0
Distributor role shifts to teammate
Sold count increases
Surry (Perfect Round)
If a team secures all 13 tricks
Called Surry
Major achievement
Meta Objective
Players track:
Number of Surry wins
Number of times opponent is SOLD
Strategic Principles
Drop weak cards early
Time strong cards for consecutive wins
Break opponent streaks
Use trump strategically
Coordinate with teammate to control flow of tricks
Team Dynamics (Important)
Even though a single player must win two consecutive tricks to secure the pile, Surry is fundamentally a team game.
Teammates support each other indirectly
A teammate can: 
Break opponent streaks
Force suit pressure
Draw out trump cards
Set up situations where their partner can win consecutive tricks
This creates a layered strategy:
Individual execution (2 consecutive wins)
Team coordination (creating those opportunities)
Teams aim to:
Disrupt opponent momentum
Enable their partner to complete streaks
Eventually dominate and achieve Surry (13 tricks)
Key Differentiators
Trick capture requires 2 consecutive wins
Same player constraint (not team)
Central pile creates tension
Persistent scoring system (pieces)
Social punishment (Distributor, SOLD)
Game Philosophy
Surry is not about winning individual tricks. It is about timing, control, and securing momentum by winning consecutive tricks at the right moment.
